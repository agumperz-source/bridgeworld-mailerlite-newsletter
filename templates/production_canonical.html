A Pair by Kantar, XI
by Edwin B. Kantar

Assume a rubber-bridge setting.

Problem 1

West leads the heart nine to South’s king. South leads jack of diamonds to the deuce, ace, and three; then a spade to his king and partner’s ten; then the spade queen, club five from partner.

Plan your defense.

Solution 1
 
NORTH
5 4 3
J 10 6
A 9 7
Q 10 9 7
EAST
A J 7 2
7 5 3
6 4 3
K 6 2
 

Contract: Four spades

Declarer is marked with 5=3=4=1, and the best chance for the defense is to force the closed hand and thereby to promote a third trump trick for the defense. To accomplish this, play the king and another club—leading a low club will limit the defense to one force in clubs.

West leads the king of diamonds, collecting the four, three, five. Partner then shifts to the king of clubs, which you overtake with the ace; declarer follows low.

Plan your defense.

Solution 2
 
NORTH

4
J 10 9 3 2
Q 3 2
A J 9 8
 

Contract: Four spades

Declarer has the ace-king of hearts, so the defense must take its diamonds now. Return the diamond deuce, ensuring that partner will win and continue the suit. (If you lead the queen, partner may overtake, placing you with queen-doubleton—from which you could not afford the queen at trick one.)
